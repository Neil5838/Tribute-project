A. P. J. Abdul Kalam

President of India

2002-2007

Avul Pakir Jainulabdeen Abdul Kalam (1931-2015), often referred to as A.P.J. Abdul Kalam, was an eminent Indian scientist and statesman who served as the 11th President of India from 2002 to 2007. Known as the "Missile Man of India" for his work on the development of ballistic missile and launch vehicle technology, Kalam played a pivotal role in India's civilian space program and military missile development.

Early Life and Education

  • Born: October 15, 1931, in Rameswaram, Tamil Nadu.
  • Family Background: Kalam came from a modest background; his father Jainulabdeen was a boat owner and imam, and his mother Ashiamma was a housewife.
  • Education: He completed his schooling at Schwartz Higher Secondary School, Ramanathapuram. He graduated in Physics from St. Joseph's College, Tiruchirappalli, and later studied aerospace engineering at the Madras Institute of Technology (MIT).

Career and Contributions

  • DRDO and ISRO: Kalam joined the Defence Research and Development Organisation (DRDO) and the Indian Space Research Organisation (ISRO). He was a part of the team that developed India's first satellite launch vehicle, SLV-III, which deployed the Rohini satellite in 1980.
  • Missile Development: He directed projects that resulted in the development of missiles like Agni and Prithvi, earning him the nickname "Missile Man."
  • Pokhran-II: In 1998, he played a critical role in the nuclear tests at Pokhran, making India a nuclear-armed state.
